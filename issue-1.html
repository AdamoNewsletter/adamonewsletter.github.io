Hello, welcome to our first edition of the ADAMO Newsletter. We'll be doing this every two weeks and we can't wait to bring you up to date with everything going on inside the ADAMO Family.

MCB & GP Content Merge
With Gaming Performance looking at developing and focussing on their new academy program, the content creator side of GP will be looking to join MCB soon to work alongside the already existing video team there. The move will also include setting up a streaming division inside the department, which will focus on organising and scheduling the ADAMO Twitch channel’s streams. This move makes sense as it allows all the video creators a centralised place to communicate and collaborate as well as allowing video creators and streamers to work together to produce streamed content or VOD style highlights from the streams.

C&E Re-Prioritising
Community and Events are looking to temporarily refocus their efforts into hosting more community driven events, such as game nights on Jackbox or Skribbl.io rather than Competitive tournaments. These events will be generally more accessible to a wider range of people due to games chosen for them and the more casual, chilled atmosphere surrounding them. Whilst the tournaments will be returning eventually, C&E are hoping to use this time to build more momentum and generate more regular interest through these activities, this also allows the staff to practice running and planning more events so that when tournaments are brought back into the picture, they will be smoother, more professional and most importantly more fun for everyone involved than previously.

Tenstar Enters Rainbow Six Siege
After seeing great success with their team in Valorant, who have recently entered the grand finals of the ESL tournament after a commanding win in the ESL Skirmish that took place throughout June, Tenstar look to broaden their scope as they announced they have thrown their hat into Rainbow Six Siege. The team was previously known as ‘Flight Esports’ who had recently achieved a promotion to Division 1 of the UK&I Nationals from a 3rd place finish. This sets the scene for Tenstar’s next move, who will again be participating in the UK&I Nationals tournament but at division 1, with the aim to work their way into the finals to prove to the rest of the world that Tenstar is an organisation to keep an eye on in more than just one game.
